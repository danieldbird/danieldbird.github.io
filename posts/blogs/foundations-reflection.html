Foundations Reflection

1st March 2022

Surprise, surprise

What surprised me most about learning core is that it is at all important. It's nice to know that a persons feelings are important in this day and age. I have in the past never had any career counselling, or guidance. I have just always assumed your mental health is your own problem, and not the concern of your peers, colleagues, or employer. It is nice and comforting to know this.

It does make sense, that you want your employees to be happy, and not only for selfless reasons. But you want your workers to be the most productive, and if their mind is not on the job because they are struggling with an issue mentally, or are burnt out, they are not going to produce quality work. Also they are more likely not to be great team members in a company.

I have learnt that I can have a conversation with people I don't know and that it is not as bad as I thought it would be. I am able to identify my ego, and attempt to keep it in check, and celebrate everyone else's achievements, and support others. I need to make sure that while I am being supportive and helpful to others, that I maintain a healthy focus on my own mental health, and my own time, to make sure the pressure of my own deadlines doesn't get overwhelming.

Meditating on meditation

I should really take time to meditate, I just never feel like doing it. I feel ok after it's done, maybe even good, but the though of sitting down and doing it I postpone and postpone, and end up seeing it like a chore.

The value of core

The role of values, empathy and self awareness is critically important when working with other people. To be kind, and generous, and to see things from another persons perspective. After all that is what I would want from others.

My challenges

The most challenging parts of core are taking the time. Taking the time to journal, reflect, meditate etc. It's challenging to see it as productive work. That at any one time it is the most important thing I could be doing. It's hard for me. But I need to work on it. It was also really challenging in the beginning to talk to people unsolicited. To make a cold call and chat about something. I find it hard to talk when I have nothing much to say, and I find it really pressurising to be given a topic and be told to talk about it for a specific period of time. I very rarely, in any other part of my life talk for more than 10 seconds continuously without another person talking back to me.

Why focus on core?

The reason for the focus on core is to produce people who are able to work and collaborate with others in a kind and productive way. It is likely one of the areas most programmers or introverts struggle with the most. Communicating and putting yourself out there. It's no good knowing everything if you cant communicate it, and therefore put it into practice.

It doesn't feel like a waste of time for me, but it can be the most laborious part for me. When I see the Tech sections I get excited, when I see the core sections I feel much less excited. I guess because things you don't know or are not good at are hard, that is why we must practice them. But sometimes hard things are not as enjoyable.
