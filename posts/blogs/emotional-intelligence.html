Emotional Intelligence

9th February 2022

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence, sometimes referred to as EQ, or EI, is a persons ability to observe their own emotions, and control them with attention and mindfulness.

It also includes a persons ability to understand and interpret somebody else‚Äôs emotions, and respond to them in a thoughtful way.

Without a practice of strengthening your emotional intelligence, it is easy to get caught in detrimental thought patterns, being self critical, and perceiving others in a way that is fundamentally untrue.

How is it different from IQ?

IQ is a measurement usually established through testing your ability to solve problems.

These problems are all academic, and not at all emotional.

A persons ability to solve academic problems, has no bearing on how they deal with their internal critical thinking, or how they empathise with other people.

Why is it important?

Learning about emotional intelligence is important because nearly everything we do in life requires interaction with other humans.

Even if you are a developer and want to only code all day, you will need to be selling whatever you are making, or talking with a manager, a client, a colleague etc.

If you are unable to work as a part of a cohesive group, more often than not people would rather not work with you no matter how good at problem solving you are.

I would rather work with somebody I can get along with and have fun solving problems together, than to be working with somebody that is unapproachable, and does not communicate in a constructive and positive way.
