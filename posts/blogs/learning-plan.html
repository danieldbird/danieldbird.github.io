Learning Plan

6th February 2022

Long term goal or career pathway

My long term goal is to either start a company that provides a software as a service product. Or to work as a developer in as part of a larger company.

I would like to use the skills I learn to understand the complete development stack from beginning to end, and apply these in any project or company I am part of in the future.

I am hoping my networking skills are greatly improved, and there may be an opportunity to find a business partner among my peers at dev academy, or network in finding a job that suits my skill level.

Strengths and limitations

My strengths are that when I am invested, and truly understand an objective, I am very good at getting there with brainstorming and persistence.

I absolutely love collaborating with others who share the same passions and who can agree on a concrete direction that we both strive toward.

I am at my strongest and most productive when I have a plan and goal for the day.

I am compassionate and empathetic by nature, and this is a great asset. However, I have to remember that my own mental health and development is really important. So I need to make sure that I am sufficiently prepared and ahead, before helping others.

My limitations are understanding complex logic without first having worked it out myself. If I am presented with code or logic somebody else has written I can struggle to understand it.

I also struggle with public speaking and being put on the spot to answer a question before I can think of an answer. I will be working hard on that during bootcamp.

Non-technical challenges

Some of my biggest non-technical challenges will be public speaking. Getting over the fear of not knowing the answer to a question when being asked in a group setting.

Another challenge will be maintaining consistency. Sticking to the plan and schedule I have set for myself. When it's time to do something I thought was a good enough idea to put in my calendar, do it.

Work on not being so self-judgemental and celebrating successes that I have. Remembering the principle that the brain is malleable, and not fixed, and I can improve what I currently struggle with.

What would I like to develop

I would like to improve my confidence with public speaking. To be comfortable not knowing something, and be able to be asked something I may not know, and be able to respond without feeling embarrassed. Not then focusing on that embarrassment, and not being present for any other questions.

I would love to develop my consistency and routine. At the moment my routine is mostly guided by inertia and whatever I think to do next, and not decisive planning.

I would like to work on improving my mental health, and the way I speak to myself internally. Being more accepting of myself, and less critical.

I also need to work on taking criticism positively instead of personally, and learn to use it to improve myself.

Expectations of the Bootcamp team

My expectations of the bootcamp team is just to be there. To be supportive if I need assistance, and to encourage me. To help me get the best out of myself and see the positives in myself.

I would like to have clear instruction as to what the challenges and goals to be achieved are broadly.

I'd also like feedback about my progress. What I have done well and what I should work to improve.

Expectations of myself

If I am stuck on a problem for a period of time without progress, I will seek help from my peers, and if I can't get clarification I will seek further coaching and instruction from facilitators.

My commitment in managing my workload during bootcamp will be to schedule everything with time blocks, and stick to that schedule.

To have a clear direction and goal for the day is key for me in being productive.

I will request and accept feedback in a positive way, and be able to provide others feedback in a way that doesn't cause tension.
